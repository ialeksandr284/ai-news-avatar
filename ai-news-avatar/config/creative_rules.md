# Creative Rules

## Creative Standard
The output should feel like a real AI news host, not a cheap AI template.

## Script Rules
- open with the consequence, not bureaucracy
- explain one thing clearly
- keep spoken density high
- remove filler transitions
- end with a clean close, not a vague summary

## Title Rules
- make the new thing obvious
- make the practical impact obvious
- avoid generic titles like `что нового в AI` too often
- rotate title patterns to avoid template fatigue
- prefer stories with broad audience appeal: new models, visual AI, video AI, voice AI, surprising product launches
- deprioritize dry infra/dev-only stories unless they have obvious mass-market impact

## Visual Rules
- clean, credible, newsroom-style presentation
- no broken lip sync
- no distracting motion
- no cheap background clutter
- no ugly watermark in paid production mode
- no visible generation bugs on face, mouth, or eyes

## Anti-Repetition Rules
- rotate intro patterns
- rotate title structures
- rotate story framing angles
- do not repeat the same cadence in every script
- do not publish multiple nearly identical `launch summary` shorts in a row

## Hook Bank Principles
Use hook families instead of one fixed formula:
- direct consequence
- speed/value delta
- builder implication
- market signal
- product shift

Bad example:
- `Компания X выпустила новую модель`

Better example:
- `Google выпустила модель, которая объединяет текст, видео и аудио в одном поисковом слое`
- `Новый релиз Google упрощает мультимодальный поиск для разработчиков`
